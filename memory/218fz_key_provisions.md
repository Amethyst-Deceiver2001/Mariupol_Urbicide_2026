## Chapter 1 — Foundational definitions (pp. 1–20)

**Art. 1 §3** — State registration is a *юридический акт признания и подтверждения* — a legal act of recognition AND confirmation of arising, modification, transfer, termination, or restriction of right on real property. Registration does not *create* the right; it *recognises* it.  
**Project use**: Cuts against occupation-side argument that the absence of a Ukrainian owner in ЕГРН means the right never existed; recognition ≠ creation.

**Art. 1 §4** — Registration is effectuated by entry of a record into ЕГРН. The *entry IS the registration* — no separate certificate needed.  
**Project use**: Explains why the bezkhoz rulings' ЕГРН entry is the final, self-executing step in the seizure lifecycle.

**Art. 1 §5** — A registered right can be challenged ONLY in court (*судебный порядок*).  
**Project use**: The occupation courts' ownerless decisions ARE the legally required judicial challenge mechanism under this article — i.e. they are the formal legal act of dispossession, not an administrative byproduct. Strengthens the argument that each court case is a discrete, documentable act of unlawful appropriation.

**Art. 7 §6** — ЕГРН records are subject to permanent storage; their destruction or removal from the register is prohibited; superseded records are preserved.  
**Project use**: Establishes chain-of-custody principle within the system itself. Occupation entries, once made, are permanent — corroborates the irreversibility of each seizure.

## Chapter 2 — ЕГРН composition and rights register content (pp. 21–40)

**Art. 8** — Cadastre content: 27 basic + 23 supplemental data fields per property object. Basic fields include cadastral number, address, area, type of object. Supplemental fields include assigned purpose, year of construction, number of floors, cadastral value, condition.  
**Project use**: Defines what data should exist for every Mariupol property in ЕГРН — absence of rights data (not absence of cadastral record) is what triggers the Art. 69 §10 ownerless notification pipeline.

**Art. 9 §3** — Rights register content includes: right type, registration number/date, right-holder identity, grounds for arising and termination, encumbrances.

**Art. 9 §3.1** — ЕГРН CAN record an *возражение* (objection) by a **previous right-holder** (предшествующий правообладатель) whose right was registered earlier, against the current registration.  
**Project use**: Mechanism available to displaced Ukrainian owners — they can formally register an objection in ЕГРН against occupation-era entries, without needing physical presence. Relevant for RD4U claims asserting A3.6 (loss of access) while ownership is contested.

**Art. 14** — Grounds for state registration include:
- п.5: Judicial acts that have entered into legal force
- п.6: Acts and certificates of property rights issued by authorized bodies under applicable law at the time of issuance  
**Project use**: п.6 is the direct basis for recognizing Ukrainian privatization certificates and БТІ registration documents as valid grounds — the occupation registry's refusal to honor these is a violation of this article's own terms (since Ukrainian law was "applicable law at time of issuance").

## Chapter 5 — Registration on specific grounds (pp. 200–219)

**Art. 58 §3** — Where a court terminates one person's right and establishes another's, registration proceeds on application of the *gaining* party without the *losing* party's consent or application, if the losing party was a defendant.  
**Project use**: This is the exact procedural mechanism used in the bezkhoz court pipeline — the municipality (gaining party) registers municipal title on its own application after the court ruling. The displaced owner (losing party/defendant) has no procedural role in the registration step. The court case IS the last mandatory legal step before ЕГРН entry.

**Art. 59** — Registration on notarially certified documents proceeds without additional verification of legal basis by the registrar.

**Art. 60** — Registration on state/municipal expropriation requires compensation documents; simultaneous cadastral de-registration and rights termination.  
**Project use**: Establishes that *lawful* expropriation requires compensation proof. Absence of compensation documentation in the occupation pipeline (where the only "ground" is the bezkhoz ruling) is per se non-compliance with this article.

**Art. 61** — ЕГРН technical and registry error correction rules. Registry errors (реестровые ошибки) must be corrected; if left more than 3 months after notification to the right-holder they can be corrected by court order.

## Chapter 10 — Liability and compensation (pp. 228–235)

**Art. 66** — State liability of the registration organ:  
§1: Organ bears liability (state treasury pays) for: (1) violations of registration rules; (2) loss or distortion of ЕГРН data; (3) unlawful refusal of registration (established by court); (4) failure to enter rights data received via interdepartmental exchange; (5) failure to enter timely; (6) entering technical errors; (7) discrepancy between public cadastral maps and ЕГРН.  
§2: Losses reimbursed **in full** from казна Российской Федерации (RF treasury).  
§3: Registration organ NOT liable for losses caused by registration based on: court-invalidated inheritance certificates or marital property certificates; void transactions applied by court.  
§4: Regress right against: (1) government body whose unlawful act caused losses; (2) cadastral surveyor who provided false data; (3) any other person whose unlawful actions caused losses.

**Art. 67** — Personal liability of the state registrar:  
§1: Personal liability for discrepancy between ЕГРН entries and submitted documents.  
§2: Must compensate the registration organ for own unlawful actions; intentional harm = full compensation.  
§3: Liable under federal law for unjustified suspension or refusal of registration.

**Art. 68** — **Compensation for loss of registered right** (Art. 68):  
§1: A natural person — owner of their **sole residence** who, through no fault of their own, cannot recover it from a bona fide purchaser — OR a bona fide purchaser from whom a sole residence was reclaimed — is entitled to a **one-time compensatory payment** (однократная компенсация) from the RF treasury.  
§2: Payment set by court judgment establishing the amount of third-party harm, if enforcement was terminated due to: (1) death of debtor-citizen; (2) exclusion of debtor-organisation from ЕГРЮЛ.  
§3: **Cap: one million rubles** (≈ €10,000 at current rates).  
§4: RF has subrogation/regress rights for sums paid.  
**Project use**: This is the Russian law's own "remedy" for loss of a registered right. The 1-million-ruble cap (§3) is grotesquely inadequate for Mariupol apartments (market value €30,000–€150,000+). Even were this remedy available to displaced Ukrainian owners (it is not — they are not in the Russian judicial system and the occupation ЕГРН does not recognise their prior registration), the inadequacy of remedy is an independent legal argument for RD4U/ECtHR claims. Furthermore, the mechanism presupposes a *bona fide purchaser* — the municipality is not one.

## Chapter 11 — Transitional provisions (pp. 235–253)

### Art. 69 — «Признание ранее возникших прав» (pp. 235–243)

This is the most critical article for the two-property-systems exhibit.

**§1**: Rights arising **before 21 July 1997** (the date 122-ФЗ came into force, establishing the prior ЕГРП register) are **legally valid without ЕГРН registration**. Such registration is *voluntary* (по желанию правообладателя).  
**Project use**: Most Mariupol apartments were privatised 1992–1997 under Ukrainian law. §1 means that even under Russian law, those pre-1997 Ukrainian privatisation rights are *юридически действительными* (legally valid) without any ЕГРН entry. The occupation system's treatment of absent ЕГРН entries as evidence of "ownerlessness" directly contradicts this article.

**§2**: Rights arising by operation of law (not on the day of ЕГРН registration, but by virtue of legal circumstances) are also legally valid without ЕГРН registration. Registration occurs on right-holder's application, or on registrar's decision upon receipt of confirming documents from state bodies or notaries.

**§3**: ЕГРН registration of previously-arising rights (§1 and §2) becomes **mandatory** when: (a) transferring those rights; (b) encumbering the property; (c) making any transaction with the property after 122-ФЗ came into force.  
**Project use**: Confirms that the occupation side's path to municipal title registration *required* first establishing what prior rights existed — which, under §1, include Ukrainian privatisation rights. The skip of this step is a procedural fraud under Russia's own law.

**§4**: Technical or state cadastral registration done before 24 July 2007 (221-ФЗ on the State Cadastre) is legally valid. Objects with rights registered in ЕГРН but without full cadastral registration — and assigned conditional numbers under 122-ФЗ — are also "ранее учтенные объекты" (previously-registered objects).

**§5**: If ЕГРН lacks information on a previously-registered object, the registration organ must ensure inclusion within **5 working days** of: a request for information, an interdepartmental query, or an application to include data. Grounds: (1) existing internal documentation; (2) documents from the right-holder establishing or confirming the right; (3) documents submitted by state/municipal bodies or technical inventory organisations on request.

**§6**: State bodies, local authorities, and technical inventory organisations must supply all available documents and data on request within **3 working days**, free of charge.

**§8**: Grounds for refusal to include a previously-registered object: (1) documents don't meet legal requirements applicable at time of issuance; (2) no data indicating the object was previously registered, and no area/dimension data; (3) the object is already in ЕГРН; (4) confirming authorities say the required documents don't exist and the applicant didn't provide them voluntarily.

**§9**: Objects whose rights arose before 122-ФЗ and have not been extinguished, but whose cadastral registration was not carried out — entered into ЕГРН under the same rules as previously-registered objects.

**§10 — THE OWNERLESS TRIGGER MECHANISM**:  
If within **5 years** of the assignment of cadastral numbers to previously-registered buildings, premises, or structures of incomplete construction, ЕГРН contains **no rights data** for those objects (nor data on proprietary rights to the land parcels they stand on), the registration organ must, within **10 working days** after expiry of that 5-year period, send information about those properties to the **уполномоченные органы местного самоуправления** (authorised local self-government organs) — i.e. the municipality.

The notification triggers the municipality's бесхозяйная вещь recognition process under ГПК гл.33 / ГК РФ ст.225.

**Project use**: THIS IS THE PIPELINE PREDICATE. Art. 69 §10 is the explicit statutory mechanism by which a property with no ЕГРН rights record becomes a bezkhoz candidate. The 5-year clock on previously-registered objects assigned cadastral numbers explains the timeline: Roskadastr mass-assigned cadastral numbers to Mariupol buildings in 2022–2023; 5-year windows would not expire until 2027–2028 under normal operation. The occupation administration's bypass — using court proceedings to DECLARE properties ownerless rather than waiting for §10 notification — is a deliberate acceleration of the statutory timeline, compressing years into months. The occupation courts are not a *supplement* to the §10 mechanism; they are a deliberate *short-circuit* of it.

## Art. 70 — Special cases (pp. 244–248)

**§3**: Land plots registered before 1 March 2008 with no rights-holder information in ЕГРН can be de-registered by the registration organ.  
**Project use**: Provides a parallel mechanism for land-plot seizure that mirrors the building pipeline.

**§5**: Until 2020, objects belonging to the Armed Forces / MoD — a cadastral declaration suffices as registration basis (no independent documentation of ownership needed).  
**Project use**: Confirms that military-use property (e.g. seized for occupation troop quartering) is on a legally distinct, expedited track within the same statute.
