# Makeshift, Ad-Hoc and Improvised Civilian Burial Sites in Mariupol (2022–2026): A Forensic Location Catalogue

## TL;DR
- The siege of Mariupol (24 Feb–20 May 2022) produced three documentarily distinct classes of burial site catalogued here: (i) hundreds of individual/courtyard makeshift graves dug beside apartment blocks, on playgrounds, in shell craters and green strips; (ii) bodies left under building rubble that became de facto mass graves; and (iii) the five organized trench cemeteries on the city outskirts. Per Human Rights Watch / SITU Research / Truth Hounds (*Beneath the Rubble*, 8 Feb 2024): "In five cemeteries in and around Mariupol between March 2022 and February 2023, we estimated at least 10,284 new burials… our count indicates that the city had at least 8,034 deaths above a peacetime rate."
- Specific, sourced locations are now geolocatable to the street address (e.g. пр. Победы 32/42; пр. Строителей 160; Солнечная 8; пр. Металлургов 96/98; ул. Киевская 53; пр. Мира 127), to named landmarks (Drama Theatre; Cheremushky "balka"; Приморський park / Nakhimov училище; the open-air morgue behind the Metro hypermarket), and to vernacular phrasings ("во дворе, в воронке от бомбы", "между гаражами", "возле детской площадки", "под турніком", "возле детсадика").
- This is an active evidence-destruction situation: from April 2022 the "DNR"/Russian Emergency Ministry began exhuming courtyard and basement bodies (often without forensic process), demolition removed bodies with construction waste, and by 2025–2026 the Manhush and Vynohradne mass-grave sites were being built over by R-280/Novorossiya highway works, with demolition rubble — likely containing human remains — crushed into road aggregate. Capturing locations now is urgent.

## Key Findings

**Scale and the basis for each estimate.** The most authoritative count is the joint **Human Rights Watch / SITU Research / Truth Hounds** investigation *Beneath the Rubble* (8 Feb 2024): by satellite analysis of five cemeteries, **at least 10,284 new burials** between March 2022 and February 2023, of which **at least 8,034 are "excess deaths"** above the peacetime rate. The **Associated Press** (Michael Biesecker, 22 Dec 2022) independently estimated **at least 10,300 new graves** across Mariupol: "An AP analysis of satellite imagery taken over the past eight months of occupation shows 8,500 new graves in the outlying Staryi Krym cemetery alone, with possibly multiple bodies beneath each mound." AP "measured each grid section where the cemetery had been expanded and calculated the total space occupied by new graves to be more than 51,500 square meters… estimating an average of roughly one grave for every six square meters," and — with methodology "confirmed by three forensic pathologists with expertise in mass graves" — concluded: "More than 10,000 new graves now scar Mariupol… and the death toll might run three times higher than an early estimate of at least 25,000."

Earlier OSINT corroborates the trajectory: **BBC Panorama**, using Centre for Information Resilience analysis of Maxar imagery (7 Nov 2022), found "1,500 new graves had been dug there since it last analysed images at the site in June. It now estimates that more than 4,600 graves have been dug there since the beginning of the war, although it says it cannot know how many bodies are buried at the site."

Higher-end figures exist and must be treated strictly as estimates with explicit attribution: Mariupol TV president **Mykola Osychenko** told Dnipro.tv (29 Aug 2022) that "as of mid-August, the Illichivskyi morgue of Mariupol documented 87,000 dead people," plus a database of 26,750 unidentified killed at the Novoazov Prosecutor's Office. The **Uppsala Conflict Data Program** range is "27,000 to 88,000 fatalities, most of them civilians." Andriushchenko and rubble-clearance workers repeatedly described **"50–100 (sometimes 80–100) bodies per destroyed building,"** and the city council reported single basements holding **100–200 dead**.

**The three location classes are distinct and must be kept separate for evidentiary purposes:**
1. *Individual / courtyard makeshift graves* — dug by residents in the first half of March 2022 because shelling made cemeteries unreachable. HRW: "During the first half of March 2022, Mariupol residents buried the bodies of their loved ones, neighbors, and strangers in shallow makeshift graves in their backyards, the courtyards of apartment blocks, and in grassy areas where children used to play."
2. *Rubble / basement de facto graves* — buildings collapsed onto sheltering civilians (Drama Theatre, Митрополитская 98, пр. Мира 127), becoming unmarked mass graves.
3. *Organized trench mass-grave sites* — exactly five, per HRW: "Starokrymske, Vynohradne, Novotroitske, Manhush, and Pavlov Street cemetery." Trench-style burial was not observed at any of these before 24 Feb 2022.

**The exhumation/clearance mechanics and timeline.** HRW: "Beginning the second week of April 2022, authorities from the 'DNR' began to clear the rubble, removing bodies they found and taking them to a makeshift morgue and then for burial in the city's cemeteries, together with bodies exhumed from makeshift gravesites." From mid-April the Mariupol City Council accused Russian forces of posting a sentry in each courtyard, exhuming courtyard graves, forbidding family reburials and removing remains to unknown locations (with unverified allegations of 13 mobile crematoria). By 10 July 2022 Andriushchenko reported exhumations resuming in high-rise courtyards (first in the пр. Свободи / Svobody Ave area), with bodies bagged and trucked to Staryi Krym as "Gruz 200." A Donetsk state corporation, **"Ритуал"/"Ritual,"** ran burials; families had to bring bodies to the makeshift morgue and, per Andriushchenko, record video stating the person was "killed by the Ukrainian army" to obtain free reburial.

**The evidence-destruction angle (war-crimes relevance).** HRW: Russia "effectively erased the physical evidence at hundreds of potential crime scenes." Митрополитская 98 was demolished by Russian firms MCT and Geostroy from June 2023. The Drama Theatre basement (the central mass-grave site) was filled with concrete in August 2022 and the ruins demolished through 2023; bodies found during clearance were trucked to Manhush "under numbers, without names." By 2025–2026 satellite imagery showed the **Manhush** mass-grave site cleared and converted to a road-repair/staging area for the R-280 "Novorossiya" highway and a 31-km Mariupol bypass; demolition rubble (≈100,000 tons of crushed stone), likely mixed with human remains, was processed at the "Ploshchadka B" landfill in the Kalmiuskyi district and sold for highway construction — Andriushchenko states this "destroyed DNA evidence."

## Details — Location Catalogue

*Convention: each entry gives the location as stated (original + English), district, date/date-range, what is known, source, and source type. "Geolocation: approximate/ungeolocated" flags vague vernacular sites. Coordinates appear ONLY where a primary source supplied them; none are invented.*

### I. Individual / courtyard makeshift graves (street-address)

**1. пр. Победы 32/42 ("банковский дом") / Peremohy (Pobedy) Ave 32/42** — shell crater in the courtyard.
- District: Left-Bank (Левобережный), on the route toward Azovstal.
- Date: from 13 March 2022 (airstrike ~09:20); burials continued to late May.
- Known: At least **16 bodies** accumulated in the bomb crater (initially 8, then "prikhoranivali" added). Diggers Yaroslav Dema and Dmytro Kuznetsov; victims include Aleksei Gospodinov (~45) and son Stepan; resident Ruslan's wife Yulia and son Roman. Bodies covered with sheet metal, not soil. MES began exhuming the crater ~5 June 2022.
- Source: Meduza, "Каждый день просыпаешься — и ты кто-то новый" (Kirill Rukov), 10 June 2022. Type: journalist investigation + eyewitness.

**2. пр. Строителей 160 / Budivelnykiv (Stroiteley) Ave 160** — two graves in the courtyard.
- District: Prymorskyi/Central (street spans districts).
- Date: 12 March 2022 (Leonid Soshenko); night of 24/25 March (Lidia Soshenko).
- Known: Taxi driver **Leonid Soshenko** (b. 1949, d. 12 Mar) buried by the Solodilov family by his building; bedridden wife **Lidia Soshenko** buried separately at the end of the building opposite entrance 6, in a "Уютный двор" landscaped strip. ### V. Organized mass-grave trench sites (outskirts)

**36. Starokrymske / Старокримське cemetery, Volodarske highway, north-west outskirts** (Mariupol's main cemetery; ~163 ha after 2011 expansion).
- Date: trench expansions from after 9 March 2022; large expansions after May 2022; growth continuing into 2024–2026.
- Known: HRW estimate **8,580 burials** (March 2022–Feb 2023); AP **~8,500**; BBC/CIR ≥4,600 by Nov 2022. Site of mass reburials of exhumed remains; concealed trench linked to пр. Мира 127 (entry 33). Plaques mostly hand-numbered, many unidentified ("в ряду из 16 могил иногда подписаны только две").
- Source: HRW; AP; Maxar; Meduza (Oct 2023). Type: satellite + investigation. Combatant share unknown.

**37. Vynohradne / Виноградне cemetery, east of Mariupol.**
- Date: new/longer trenches first visible end of March 2022 (Maxar: 22–29 Mar); ~40-m parallel trenches.
- Known: HRW estimate **864 burials**. Early-April estimates of ≥1,000 (a 45×25 m grave). By 2025–2026 partly obscured by road construction.
- Source: HRW; Maxar via Axios/The Hill/NPR; Kyiv Independent (2025). Type: satellite + investigation. Combatant share unknown.

**38. Novotroitske / Новотроїцьке cemetery, north-east of Mariupol (~2.5 km NE of Left Bank).**
- Date: 480 new individual graves, most visible by first week of March 2022; new sectors flagged by Andriushchenko 26 Apr 2023 (some larger than Vynohradne/Manhush trenches — possibly Russian military dead).
- Known: HRW estimate **480 burials**. Reburial destination (e.g. Arina Antipenko reburied here).
- Source: HRW; Andriushchenko via Radio Svoboda. Type: satellite + official.

**39. Pavlov Street cemetery / кладовище на вул. Павлова, northern outskirts.**
- Date: 45 new individual graves, all March–12 May 2022.
- Known: HRW estimate **45 burials** (likely undercount; vegetation/angle limited analysis).
- Source: HRW. Type: satellite. Combatant share unknown.

**40. Manhush / Мангуш cemetery, ~20 km west of Mariupol.**
- Date: trench expansion 23–26 March through April 2022; 315 individual graves counted by 12 Oct 2022.
- Known: HRW counted **315 new individual graves**; the early-April satellite/city-council claim of a 300-m+ trench holding **up to 9,000** bodies (Maxar: >200 graves, four ~85-m sections) is a city-council estimate, not a verified count. Bodies trucked from the Drama Theatre and elsewhere. By 2025–2026 the site was cleared and converted to a road-repair staging area (R-280 highway/bypass) — a documented evidence-destruction event.
- Source: HRW; Maxar via NPR/NBC/Space.com; Mariupol City Council; Kyiv Independent / UNITED24 (2025–2026). Type: satellite + official. Combatant share unknown.

## Recommendations

1. **Immediate (geolocation sprint).** Prioritize the street-address courtyard graves in Section I for precise geolocation against pre-war imagery now, because (a) they are the most evidentiary class (named victims, datable causes) and (b) they are being exhumed/built over fastest. ## Caveats
- **No coordinates or addresses have been invented.** Where a source gave only vernacular phrasing or a neighbourhood, the entry is flagged "approximate/ungeolocated." Several counts (Будівельників 189 ≈11; Троїцька ≈20; Manhush up to 9,000; Oktyabr ~70; Drama Theatre toll) are eyewitness or official **estimates**, not verified exhumation counts, and are labelled as such.
- **Combatant vs civilian:** HRW repeatedly states it is unclear whether any of those interred in the five trench cemeteries were combatants; the 10,284 figure is "an unknown number of soldiers among them." Andriushchenko notes some Novotroitske sectors may hold Russian military dead.
- **Occupation/Russian-state sources** (DAN/dan-news.ru, URA.RU, RIA Novosti, Lenta.ru, mariupol-news) are used only for cross-reference and are explicitly labelled; their framing ("nationalists," "liberation") is propaganda and their body-cause attributions ("killed by the Ukrainian army," coerced on video) are unreliable.
- **Single-source civilian-channel entries** (e.g. mariupolRIP posts) carry PII and should be corroborated before any public filing; handle victims' names with dignity.
- **The catalogue is a floor, not a ceiling.** HRW, AP and the City Council all stress hundreds of courtyard/stikhiyni graves remain undocumented, many bodies remain under rubble, and some remains were destroyed with demolition waste and will never be counted. This document should be treated as a living catalogue.
