# Mariupol Urbicide 2026 – Research Aggregation (June 2026, Rev. 2)

## Overview

This document aggregates key research findings for the Mariupol Urbicide 2026 project as of late June 2026. It consolidates answers to targeted open questions about Russian federal programmes, DNR/occupation-era legal instruments, municipal practice, and systemic obstacles to property restitution, with full provenance for each claim and explicit links to primary sources where available.

## Q1. Founding Decree for 2% Subsidized Mortgage in New Regions

### Instrument Identification

The founding legal instrument for the 2% subsidized mortgage programme in the occupied regions (DNR, LNR, Zaporizhzhia, Kherson) is:

> Постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 31 декабря 2022 г. № 2565 "Об утверждении Правил предоставления субсидий из федерального бюджета акционерному обществу "ДОМ.РФ" в виде вкладов в имущество акционерного общества "ДОМ.РФ", не увеличивающих его уставный капитал, на цели возмещения российским кредитным и иным организациям недополученных ими доходов по кредитам (займам), выданным гражданам Российской Федерации для приобретения или строительства жилых помещений на территориях Донецкой Народной Республики, Луганской Народной Республики, Запорожской и Херсонской областей, а также на территориях иных субъектов Российской Федерации …".[cite:32]

Primary and authoritative sources:

- **Government portal**: `[Постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 31.12.2022 № 2565](http://government.ru/docs/all/145675/)`.[cite:49]
- **Legal aggregator**: GARANT reproduction of the full text and title (paywalled, but summary confirms scope and wording).[cite:32]

### Programme Parameters and Press Confirmation

Contemporary press reporting (January 2023) corroborates the programme parameters and associates them with Mishustin's signed постановление:

- Residents of DNR, LNR, Zaporizhzhia and Kherson oblasts can obtain housing loans at **2% annual interest**.[cite:17][cite:24][cite:29]
- Maximum loan amount **6 million rubles**, minimum down payment **10%**.[cite:17][cite:24]
- Loans may be used for purchase or construction of housing in the listed territories.[cite:17][cite:20]
- Multiple outlets (RIA Novosti, Gazeta.ru, Komsomolskaya Pravda) describe the arrangement as a programme "утвердил Кабмин" / "утвердило Правительство", signed by Mikhail Mishustin and set out in a Government постановление.[cite:17][cite:20][cite:24][cite:29]

### Relationship to Amendment № 2166 and Later Acts

Later Government acts treat № 2565 as the founding baseline:

- A Government news item on постановление от 8 июля 2023 г. № 1123 references "внесены изменения в постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 31 декабря 2022 г. № 2565", confirming that № 2565 is the original programme being amended.[cite:18]
- Your project has separately captured Постановление Правительства РФ от 15.12.2023 № 2166 (amendment), which modifies the same scheme.

№ 2565 (31.12.2022) should therefore be treated as the founding instrument that launched the 2% mortgage programme in the occupied regions; all later amendments and sector coverage build on this act.

## Q2. Постановление № 2501 (29.12.2022) – Federal vs Municipal Property Demarcation

### Instrument Identification and Title

The instrument cited in Law № 269-РЗ as the basis for routing some municipal/"ownerless" property into federal ownership is:

> Постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 29 декабря 2022 г. № 2501 "Об утверждении особенностей управления и распоряжения отдельными объектами имущества, расположенными на территориях Донецкой Народной Республики, Луганской Народной Республики, Запорожской области и Херсонской области, находящимися в государственной или муниципальной собственности, а также разграничения имущества между Российской Федерацией, каждым из указанных субъектов Российской Федерации и его муниципальными образованиями".[cite:61][cite:62]

Primary and authoritative sources:

- **Official publication record**: `[Постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 29.12.2022 № 2501](http://publication.pravo.gov.ru/Document/View/0001202212300029)` – includes the full title, publication number **0001202212300029**, and publication date (30.12.2022).[cite:184]
- **Government portal**: entry for Постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 29.12.2022 № 2501, with text and annexes.[cite:63]
- **Legal aggregators**: GARANT and continent-online mirrors confirming title, scope, and that the act is later amended by постановления № 1911 (26.12.2024) and subsequent acts.[cite:61][cite:62][cite:67][cite:65]

### Scope of Regulation

The постановление establishes a framework for managing and demarcating property on the occupied territories:

- Article 1: sets out that the document establishes features of management and disposal of certain property objects located on the territories of DNR, LNR, Zaporizhzhia, Kherson, and features of demarcation of property between the Russian Federation, each of these subjects, and their municipal formations.[cite:62]
- Article 2: states that the act enters into force upon official publication and is effective until 1 January 2027 (with shorter validity for specific subparagraphs).[cite:62][cite:184]

It differentiates between property that must be federal (Appendix № 1) and property that may be federal, regional or municipal (Appendix № 2).[cite:61]

### Treatment of Housing – Appendix № 2

Housing enters the framework via the list of property categories that "may" be assigned to different levels of ownership:

- Appendix № 2, item 9: "Жилищный фонд, имущество жилищно-эксплуатационных и ремонтно-строительных предприятий."[cite:63]

This places housing stock (including multi-unit buildings and the assets of maintenance/repair enterprises) in the class of property whose ownership can be determined by decision, rather than being automatically federal.

### Mechanism for Routing Housing (Including "Ownerless" Housing) to Federal Ownership

The act does not mention "бесхозяйное" property explicitly; instead it sets procedures by which categories of property, including housing, can be assigned to federal, regional or municipal ownership.

Key mechanisms:

- Article 4:
  - Property listed in Appendix № 1 is assigned to federal ownership.
  - Property listed in Appendix № 2 may be assigned to federal ownership, the subject's ownership, or municipal ownership.[cite:61]

- Article 6:
  - Federal Agency for State Property Management (Росимущество) makes decisions on assignment of objects or categories listed in Appendix № 2 to subject-level or municipal ownership, in agreement with a collegiate body.[cite:61]

- Article 6¹:
  - A collegiate body can:
    - Assign categories listed in Appendix № 1 to federal ownership; 
    - Assign categories listed in Appendix № 2 to federal, subject, or municipal ownership.[cite:61]

- Article 7:
  - After demarcation, property in Appendix № 2 may be transferred between federal, subject, and municipal ownership.[cite:61]

- Article 19:
  - Property currently in subject or municipal ownership can be transferred to federal ownership if it is needed for:
    - Implementation of international or nationwide measures and coordination of federal and regional bodies;
    - Execution of powers or activities of federal bodies or their subordinate organisations;
    - Ensuring the livelihoods of the population and preserving technological unity and safe operation of facilities.[cite:61]

Procedural details:

- Articles 11–12¹: proposals to assign property to subject or municipal ownership, or to transfer Appendix № 2 property between levels, are made via a "декларация об объекте имущества" and submitted to Росимущество.[cite:61][cite:63]
- Articles 14–14¹: describe how Rosimushchestvo and the collegiate body review and decide on such proposals.[cite:61][cite:63]

### Interpretation in Relation to Law № 269-РЗ

In the context of Mariupol's "бесхозяйное" housing chain under Law № 269-РЗ:

- Law № 269-РЗ provides the mechanism by which certain housing is declared "бесхозяйное" and moved into municipal or state ownership (outside the scope of постановление № 2501 itself).
- Постановление № 2501 then provides the federal-level framework that allows housing in the municipal/subject ownership (including "ownerless" houses recognized by № 269-РЗ) to be assigned or transferred to federal ownership, via Appendices № 2 and Articles 6¹, 7, 19.

Thus, № 2501 is a structural legal basis for the branch of the property-transfer chain in which "ownerless" municipal housing can end up as federal property.

## Q3. Annex 4 to Госпрограмма № 2255 – Per-M² Compensation Rate

### Instrument Identification

The federal programme cited in Law № 269-РЗ as the source for per-square-metre monetary compensation is:

> Постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 22.12.2023 № 2255 "Об утверждении государственной программы Российской Федерации "Восстановление и социально-экономическое развитие Донецкой Народной Республики, Луганской Народной Республики, Запорожской области и Херсонской области"".[cite:73][cite:81]

Primary and authoritative sources:

- **Official publication record**: `[Постановление Правительства Российской Федерации от 22.12.2023 № 2255](http://publication.pravo.gov.ru/document/0001202312290053?index=67)` – confirms number, date, and title.[cite:84]
- **Legal aggregators**: GARANT and CNTD provide reproductions of the programme text and annex structure.[cite:73][cite:81]

### Annex 4 – Rules for Compensation

Annex 4 (Приложение № 4) contains rules for providing and distributing certain subsidies, including those for lost housing. The exact per-m² figures are deduced from a Government-cited amendment project:

- A draft постановление (GARANT) proposes amendments to point 14 of Annex 4 and enumerates the current values being replaced:

> «Внести в пункт 14 приложения N 4 ... следующие изменения:
> а) в абзаце пятом цифры "51,5" заменить цифрами "70";
> б) в абзаце восьмом цифры "50" заменить цифрами "55", цифры "100" заменить цифрами "110".»[cite:79]


This draft and explanatory materials indicate that these values correspond to:

- Per-m² compensation for lost housing: **51.5 thousand rubles per square metre** (planned increase to 70 thousand).[cite:79][cite:75]
- Lump-sum amounts:
  - **50 thousand rubles per person** for partially lost essential property.
  - **100 thousand rubles per person** for totally lost essential property.[cite:79]

Because the amendment explicitly modifies Annex 4 and lists the pre-existing numbers, the original Annex 4, as approved on 22.12.2023, is understood to have set:

- 51,500 ₽/m² as the baseline per-m² compensation rate for lost housing.
- 50,000 ₽ and 100,000 ₽ per person brackets for partial/total property loss.

### Provenance and Limitations

- The primary text of Annex 4 is not directly exposed in the snippet of the publication record on `publication.pravo.gov.ru`, but the Government-linked amendment draft and GARANT reproductions provide a strong evidentiary chain.[cite:79][cite:84][cite:73]
- RIA and other coverage of the proposal to raise compensation to 70,000 ₽/m² explicitly note that current compensation is 51,500 ₽/m², aligning with the Annex 4 baseline.[cite:75]

For Law № 269-РЗ Art. 7(3), Annex 4 to госпрограмма № 2255 should be treated as the source that sets the monetary compensation at 51,500 ₽ per m² of lost housing, with the specified per-person lump sums, until amended.

## Q5. East SOS / Filtration Entry-Ban Dataset

### HRW’s Use of East SOS and Russian Official Figures

The ~30,000 denied-entry figure and 20–50-year ban durations are derived from Russian authorities’ reporting, as quoted by Human Rights Watch, not from an East SOS dataset.

In a May 2026 HRW article on Russia illegally seizing property in occupied areas, mirrored on ECOI:

- East SOS filtration ratio:

> "According to Ukraine's civil society group East SOS, only one out of every four people undergoing this 'filtration' process is allowed to proceed."[cite:90]

- Russian authorities’ denied-entry figures:

> "Russian authorities reported that between October 2023 and April 2025, **30,000 Ukrainians were denied entry and issued entry bans ranging from 20 to 50 years**."[cite:90]

Provenance:

- ECOI’s mirror of the HRW article retains the citations and wording; HRW states explicitly that these are figures reported by Russian authorities.[cite:90]

### East SOS’s Own Publication – October 2023 Appeal

The East SOS publication most relevant to entry restrictions is:

- `APPEAL on entry restrictions for Ukrainian citizens from third countries by the Russian Federation` (East SOS, October 16, 2023).[cite:102]

Primary source:

- East SOS English-language page: `[APPEAL on entry restrictions for Ukrainian citizens from third countries by the Russian Federation](https://east-sos.org/en/publications/appeal-on-entry-restrictions-for-ukrainian-citizens-from-third-countries-by-the-russian-federation/)`.[cite:102]

Key content:

- Describes the Russian Government’s decision to restrict entry for Ukrainian citizens arriving from third countries to very limited checkpoints (Sheremetyevo Airport and certain land crossings, later effectively reduced to Sheremetyevo).[cite:92][cite:102]
- Highlights impacts on Ukrainians seeking to return to occupied territories or reunite with family.

It does **not** contain numeric counts of denied entries or ban lengths.

### OHCHR HRMMU and MIHR

OHCHR’s thematic report on detention and filtration in Ukraine (June 2023) and public-facing materials from the Media Initiative for Human Rights provide narrative descriptions of filtration and rights violations, but no discrete dataset reproducing the 30,000 denied entries or 20–50-year bans.

- OHCHR document: `2023-06-27-Ukraine-thematic-report-detention-ENG.pdf` provides qualitative analysis of detention and filtration, without cumulative ban numbers.[cite:115]

### Evidentiary Status for the Project

For the project’s S7 "sham process" context:

- The **30,000 / 20–50-year ban figures** should be attributed to **Russian authorities**, citing the HRW article as the source that reports them.[cite:90]
- The **1-in-4 filtration success ratio** should be attributed to **East SOS**, again via HRW’s citation.[cite:90]
- East SOS’s October 2023 appeal should be used as primary context for entry restrictions and route closures, not as a dataset.[cite:102]

No public structured dataset is currently available from East SOS, OHCHR, or MIHR that could be joined at person/incident level to the property chain; these statistics remain systemic context only.
