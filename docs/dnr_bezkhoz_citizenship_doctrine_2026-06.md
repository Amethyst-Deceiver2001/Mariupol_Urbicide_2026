# The DNR Supreme Court's "abandonment-by-displacement" doctrine

*Analysis date: 2026-06-27. Built by reading the full reasoning text — not
just outcomes — of the Mariupol-property cases in the captured DNR Supreme
Court "ownerless property" population (`scripts/175`/`176`,
`docs/dnr_bezkhoz_appellate_outcomes_2026-06.md`). Raw rulings:
`data/raw/<sha>.html`. This document quotes the occupation court's own words.*

## The finding in one sentence

The DNR Supreme Court has articulated, **in writing and repeatedly**, a
doctrine that treats a Ukrainian citizen's flight from the siege of Mariupol —
combined with their not having taken a Russian passport — as a **voluntary
abandonment** of their home, and on that basis vests the home in the
occupation municipality, **expressly disregarding that the family still lives
there and pays every utility bill, and expressly holding that the owner's
attempt to claim the property does not even create a legal dispute.**

A statistical disparity in the original 95-case sample (Mariupol owners
losing appeals at ~2.5× the rate of owners elsewhere in occupied DNR) was
revised twice on 2026-06-27 — see the correction notice in
[dnr_bezkhoz_appellate_outcomes_2026-06](dnr_bezkhoz_appellate_outcomes_2026-06.md):
first to 1.28×/p=0.49 (full-population re-pull), then to 1.5×/p=0.29 after a
classifier bug fix moved the estimate back toward the original reading.
Current status: directionally plausible, not statistically proven. None of
this weakens this finding: the doctrine below is established by **direct
reading of specific ruling text**, not by a population-level rate, and
reading the rulings shows it is applied to displaced Ukrainian owners and
withheld from those who can document Russian citizenship or personally
assert their claim through occupation channels — regardless of how common or
rare that pattern turns out to be at full-docket scale.

## The doctrine, element by element, in the court's own words

Every quotation below is from a captured, hashed ruling (hashes in the table
at the end). `<данные изъяты>` / `<адрес>` are the court's own redactions.

1. **Citizenship is named as the predicate.** «…документирована паспортом
   гражданина Украины, являющегося недружественным иностранным государством…
   паспортом гражданина Российской Федерации не документирована» (33-2342/2025).
   In 33-2875/2025 the operative holding literally calls her «гражданина
   Украины Ягмурджи Е.А.» while finding she has no rights to defend.

2. **Flight from the war is recharacterised as abandonment of possession.**
   «…устранилась от владения и пользования принадлежащим ей имуществом,
   выехала на территорию недружественного иностранного государства — Украину,
   проживает в Польше, не принимает меры по содержанию спорного имущества»
   (33-2342/2025). The police report in that same case records that the owner
   «во время боевых действий уехала в Польшу» — she fled the fighting.

3. **The registration ban is cited as part of the justification — the closed
   loop.** The rulings cite Указ Президента РФ №1103 (24.12.2024) and №145
   (14.03.2025), which forbid a hostile-state citizen from registering property
   rights without special permission, and treat that very prohibition as
   confirming the owner cannot assert title. A Ukrainian owner is thus: barred
   from registering → therefore deemed to hold no recognised title → therefore
   deemed to have abandoned → therefore dispossessed. 33-2875/2025 makes the
   loop explicit: her Ukrainian notarial title needs "legalisation," she is
   barred (as a Ukrainian) from obtaining it, **therefore** the seizure «не
   нарушает её прав» and she lacks standing even to appeal.

4. **Paid utilities are expressly held not to count.** «Факт оплаты таких услуг
   иным лицом вместо собственника сам по себе не свидетельствует о выполнении
   собственником обязанностей, связанных с бременем содержания своего
   имущества» (33-2769/2025). In 33-2342/2025 the court catalogues gas, water
   and communal accounts all paid and current, then seizes anyway.

5. **An owner appearing to claim the home is held NOT to create a dispute.**
   «Само по себе выявление лиц, претендующих на признание за ними права на
   имущество, не свидетельствует о наличии спора» (33-2769/2025). This is the
   precise inversion of the rule applied in the rest of DNR, where the
   existence of a claimant triggers «спор о праве» and bounces the petition to
   ordinary proceedings. In Mariupol displacement cases, the claimant is read
   out of existence.

6. **The objective — transfer to the state — is stated openly, and resisting
   it is branded obstruction.** The court characterises an owner-heir's defence
   of his flat as «намерение… воспрепятствовать передачи такого имущества в
   собственность государства в обход действующего в переходный период
   законодательства» (33-2769/2025; identical language in 33-1509/2025). The
   court frames keeping your own home as circumventing the law.

## The control group: how owners WIN, and the one Ukrainian who won

The WIN cases (petition bounced or refused) contain **none** of the doctrine
markers. They turn on one of: the owner **personally appeared** (33-689,
33-2632, 33-306); the owner could document **Russian** citizenship/registration
(33-2283); or the court found a genuine «спор о праве» on ordinary grounds.

The decisive control is **33-526/2026**: the owner **is** a Ukrainian citizen
(«гражданка недружественного государства — Украины») living in **Germany** — yet
she WON. Why: she had a documented illness explaining why she could not appear
in Mariupol in person, and she **actively asserted her claim through the
Russian embassy in Germany**. That active assertion created the dispute that
saved the flat. The lesson is not that citizenship is harmless; it is that the
abandonment presumption is **rebuttable only by active assertion through
Russian/occupation channels** — a bar a displaced civilian who fled to
Ukraine, or who lacks the health, money, or documents to work the Russian
consular system from abroad, cannot clear. For most, the presumption is
decisive.

## Named actors (occupation officials, in scope for accountability)

Judges are public officials acting in official capacity and are **not**
minimised (CLAUDE.md privacy rule). Across these Mariupol rulings, the
**seizure-affirming** panels and the **petition-bouncing** panels diverge by
composition:

- **Judge Бескровная Е.Л.** sat on the panel in 4 of the 5 doctrine cases
  (33-2342, 33-2769, 33-2875, and others) — the most consistent presence on
  the seizure-affirming side.
- **Presiding judges Дементьева О.С. and Олейникова В.В.** presided over the
  doctrine seizures (Дементьева: 33-2875, 33-2769; Олейникова: 33-2342,
  33-1873, 33-994).
- By contrast, **presiding judge Храпин Ю.В.**'s panels produced the WIN/bounce
  outcomes across the board (33-2720, 33-2632, 33-2283, 33-689, 33-641) — an
  intra-court split worth noting, though per-judge N is small.
- First-instance Mariupol judges whose grants were *affirmed*: Мяконькая Т.А.,
  Митерев Э.Е., Белоусов П.В. (Жовтневый/Орджоникидзевский districts).

## Why this matters for the project's two endpoints

- **Rome Statute.** This is the occupier's own appellate court, in writing,
  operating a doctrine that strips homes from civilians **on the basis of their
  (Ukrainian) nationality and their displacement by the very hostilities the
  occupier initiated** — directly relevant to persecution (art. 7(1)(h)) and to
  unlawful appropriation of property not justified by military necessity
  (art. 8(2)(a)(iv)). The "transfer… в собственность государства" language and
  the branding of self-defence as "obstruction" are admissions of intent.
- **RD4U.** Each ruling is a dated, self-incriminating A3.6 (loss of access)
  record naming a specific property, a specific dispossession, and specific
  occupation officials — and, unusually, one in which the deprivation is
  reasoned aloud rather than hidden.

## Caveats

- Sample is the captured Mariupol-flagged subset (17 cases; ~5 residential
  LOSE). The doctrine is unambiguous in those; the statistical disparity around
  it is weaker and was twice-revised — current reading **1.5×, Fisher *p* =
  0.29** (see the header and
  [dnr_bezkhoz_appellate_outcomes_2026-06](dnr_bezkhoz_appellate_outcomes_2026-06.md)),
  not the 0.057 an earlier draft of this caveat quoted. The doctrine finding
  rests on the ruling text, not on the p-value.
- This is still the **appellate, contested** layer — the uncontested
  first-instance grants (the bulk of seizures) are documented separately in
  [dnr_district_first_instance_2026-06](dnr_district_first_instance_2026-06.md),
  which finds an **87.1%** first-instance grant rate and that ~⅓ of granted
  seizures had a living owner named in the case file.
- `<данные изъяты>` redactions mean the owner's exact nationality is sometimes
  inferred from the legal mechanism (Указ №1103/145 apply only to hostile-state
  citizens) rather than stated in plain text; where the text says «Украины»
  outright this is noted.
