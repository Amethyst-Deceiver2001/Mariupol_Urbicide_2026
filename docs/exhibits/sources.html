01 · Primary government sources

Occupation court portals

ГАС «Правосудие» (the Russian state judicial-records system), mirrored onto DNR domains. Each case is a self-incriminating, dated lifecycle record: utility cut-off → “ownerless” notice → inspection → designation → municipal-ownership petition → transfer. This case type is now closed — ФКЗ-4 (15 Dec 2025) abolished the court stage in favor of direct registry inclusion.

03 · Telegram

Telegram

~190,000 individually hashed captures across 37 distinct channels, split into two categories with different publication rules.

28 building-chat resident channels (~145,000 messages)

These are private or semi-private resident chat groups for specific Mariupol apartment buildings — the project’s primary-source intelligence on day-to-day occupation, seizures, evictions, and demolition as residents experienced them. Per this project’s privacy rule, invite links to these private chats are not republished here; each channel is documented internally against the property it covers (src/mariupol_seizures/chat_buildings.py) and cited address-by-address in the relevant case studies. 07 · Legal & journalistic context

International legal frameworks & reporting

Not crawled sources — the legal and journalistic record this project's findings are mapped against, and that supplied the original investigative lead.

  • Human Rights Watch“Ukraine: Russia Illegally Seizing Property in Occupied Areas,” 26 May 2026 — ~8,116 cases across 25 occupation court sites, the originating lead for the court-docket stream
  • UN OHCHR43rd periodic report on Ukraine, 9 Dec 2025
  • Council of Europe — Register of Damage for Ukrainerd4u.coe.int — RD4U claim categories (A3.1/A3.2/A3.3/A3.6) used to classify every property on the spine
  • Rome Statute, International Criminal CourtArt. 8(2)(b)(viii) — transfer of the occupier’s own population; the criminal-accountability framework this archive is structured to feed
  • BBC Verify, Kyiv Independent, Euromaidan Press, NV, CEPA, Mezha, Russian Life / Dossier Center, rg.ru, mrpl.news2025–2026 reporting consulted for context and cross-referencing; full citation list in docs/reconceptualization_2026.md

A note on completeness

This list reflects the project’s chain-of-custody log as of the most recent capture run. It will grow as new sources are crawled — the live front line, as of mid-2026, is weekly re-snapshotting of the ownerless registry ahead of the 1 July 2026 deadline. Nothing here is presented as exhaustive of what exists, only of what has been captured, hashed, and is on file.
Occupation government records (court rulings, registry entries, decrees) are catalogued here as evidence of the act of seizure, never as valid title. Ukraine does not recognize them; neither does this project.
