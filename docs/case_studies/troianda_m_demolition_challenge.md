# Case Study — HOA (TSZh) «Troianda-M»: the occupation's own courts wrote the demolish-and-resell playbook into the record while denying it

**Sixty-three residents and their homeowners' association spent nearly two years
losing the same case three times in occupation courts. Along the way, a judge
read into the record a demolition contract dated two years *after* the
building was already rubble. The replacement project has a name, a federal
backer, and — as of this session — a confirmed address: prosp. Metallurgov,
47, Mariupol.**

This is a different modality from the two already documented in this project.
`nakhimova_82_chernomorsky_1b.md` tracks one demolished footprint through
official registries with no resistance from the former owners. The mass-resale
case study (`mass_registry_to_resale.md`) tracks intact buildings stripped flat
by flat through the "ownerless" registry. Troianda-M is the only documented
instance where the dispossessed **fought back through the occupier's own legal
system** — and lost at every level, which is precisely what makes the case
file useful: a court record is harder to dismiss as activist reconstruction
when the occupier wrote it.

**Update, 2026-06-26 (address resolved):** the user supplied a set of press,
Telegram, and video sources independently naming the address as **prosp.
Metallurgov, 47** (sometimes recorded "47/2"). This retires the prior "Dom s
chasami"/Lenina-corner hypothesis entirely — that was a different, sibling
parcel under the same decree, never this case's building. It also surfaces a
federal-level backer for the replacement project and a detailed,
self-incriminating account of the demolition's paper trail, read into the
court record by a resident in June 2025. See "The address, now confirmed"
and "What the resident's own video puts on the record," below.

caught and fixed before they could distort this project's own floor
estimate. Consistent with this project's standing rule against trusting a
single automated extraction without a sanity check (see
`memory/negation_blind_classifier_caveat`).

## What the ruling documents, in its own words

Court rulings are usually read for their holding. This one is more useful read
for its findings of fact — because the appellate court, explaining why the
residents lose, had to lay out the full administrative sequence that produced
their loss. Everything in this table is drawn directly from the captured
ruling text (case 33-2575/2025):

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| Feb 2022 | Building sustains partial combat damage during the siege; residents continue living in it and its basement afterward |
| 23.07.2022 | GKO DNR Resolution №162 establishes the demolition-assessment procedure for war-damaged buildings |
| 06.04.2022 | GKO DNR Resolution №1 — the framework cited for assigning the contractor/inspection roles on the commission (note: dated *before* №162, cited as background authority) |
| 03.08.2022 | Technical-survey report issued — jointly attributed in the case file to two different contractors: OOO «Professionalnye ekspertnye tekhnologii» and OOO IB «StroiTekh-XXI» |
| 29.09.2022 | Commission conclusion №118 declares the building unfit for habitation and condemns it for demolition |
| **10–25.12.2022** | Building physically demolished — dated precisely by residents' own video evidence submitted to the court (see below), not just "Dec 2022" |
| 26.07.2023 | Formal act recording completed demolition works, naming contractor **OOO «RKS-NR»** as the party that carried out the dismantling |
| 07.09.2023 | Decree of the Head of the DNR №291 grants OOO SZ «RKS-Development» (ИНН 9310007980) a *number of* municipal land parcels, without auction — one of them 3,136 m², at the intersection of prosp. Metallurgov and a redacted street, for investment project **«Novoe vremya 2»** |
| **27.12.2024** | A demolition CONTRACT between PPK «Edinyi zakazchik» and RKS-NR, for ~103 million RUB — disclosed by the judge in April 2025, dated **two years after** the physical demolition (see below) |
| 28.10.2024 | Lawsuit filed |

Two details in the ruling itself do the self-incriminating work. First, the
technical reports and the commission's own conclusion list **three different
commission rosters across three separate documents** — a composition
irregularity the governing procedure (Resolution №162) does not allow, raised
by residents on appeal. Second, the report underlying the demolition decision
(StroiTekh-XXI's technical assessment) was challenged by residents for
lacking an approval signature — the appellate court considered this but
upheld the lower court's reliance on it anyway.

The court also confirms that before TSZh «Troianda-M» re-registered in 2024,
the demolished property had been formally "assigned to" **PPK «Edinyi
zakazchik»** — i.e. the property passed through the PPK's custody during the
gap between demolition and the residents' association regaining legal
standing to sue over it. The appellate court used exactly this gap to deny
the claim: the PPK's custodial assignment, it held, predates the residents'
association's legal standing to contest it.

## Compensation framework cited — and not delivered

The ruling cites GKO DNR Resolution №175 (30.07.2022), §5.3 of which entitles
residents of a demolished building to compensation housing **in the building
constructed on the same site**. The residents' claim rested partly on this.
The administration's own correspondence in the case file (response
№Н-1284-17) describes the replacement project as still under construction,
with works scheduled through 2025 — i.e., the administration's own answer to
the residents did not deny a building was being built on the site; it simply
said it wasn't ready yet. The appellate court denied the claim regardless, on
the PPK-custody-predates-standing grounds described above.

## Why this case matters

Every other case study in this project documents a seizure the occupier
recorded without resistance. This one documents what happens when residents
use the only system the occupier left them — and the system's own paperwork
becomes the most detailed account of the seizure that exists, even though it
denies them everything. Between the ruling and the resident's own video, the
record now shows: a condemnation process run with three inconsistent
commission rosters; a technical report a resident specifically flagged as
procedurally defective; a contractor relationship formalized by contract two
years after the physical work it supposedly authorized, for roughly 103
million rubles; and a compensation-housing entitlement the administration's
own correspondence concedes was unmet at the time of the ruling, against a
compensation rate that undershoots the local market by some 15% even when
paid.

It also establishes something a damage assessment or a registry entry
cannot: **exhaustion of domestic remedies**, now extended past the federal
cassation denial to a further Supreme Court cassation filing (27.02.2026, per
press). That exhaustion is the procedural precondition restitution
mechanisms — and the European Court of Human Rights' own jurisprudence going
